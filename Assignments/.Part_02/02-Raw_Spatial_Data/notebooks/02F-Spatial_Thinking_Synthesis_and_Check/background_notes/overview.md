Spatial thinking is not about tools, software, or file formats.

It is about **how you reason about space**.

This lesson consolidates ideas from the entire Raw Spatial Data module
into a single conceptual framework.

---

## What Spatial Thinking Really Means

Spatial thinking involves:

- understanding reference and context
- recognizing assumptions
- reasoning about scale and detail
- editing data intentionally
- questioning results critically

These ideas apply regardless of tools or technology.

---

## Why This Lesson Exists

Before moving on to advanced topics, students must be able to:

- explain spatial reasoning in words
- identify flawed assumptions
- connect concepts across lessons

This lesson ensures conceptual readiness.

---

## Key Takeaway

> **Spatial thinking is a habit, not a technique.**
